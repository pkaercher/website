YOU FOUND THE PORK TONKATSU!

This is my favorite meal, and it takes about 30 minutes to make.

To eat the pork tonkatsu..

  • Pound boneless pork chops as thin as possible. I don't have a meat pounder, so I flatten them by putting the wide part of a large blade flat against the pork and pound it with the butt of my hand. Then I whack the chops with the edge of the blade to make really shallow cuts in a crosshatch.
  • Salt and pepper the chops on both sides.
  • Cover them in flour, then beaten egg, then panko break crumbs.
  • Fry them a cast iron pan, or any pan, over medium heat with a generous amount of oil until dark golden brown and 165 deg inside, about 4 minutes on each side.
  • Let the pork chops rest on a cutting board for 5-10 minutes, then cut into 3/4 inch slices.
  • Serve the pork with gochujan (red chili paste), like Mother-In-Law sauce - you can hopefully find it in a jar in the Asian food aisle.
I serve this over rice. Recently I started making rice with coconut milk instead of water, and it's delicious.
  • Before cooking rinse the rice until the water is clear. Drain.
  • Shake the rice in the pan so it forms a level surface, and then pour in enough coconut milk so that the surface of the coconut milk is nearly an inch above the surface of the rice.
  • Cook over medium heat with the lid on until it starts to boil, then turn the burner to low. Shut the burner off once you can no longer see any liquid bubbling through the rice (about 10 minutes), but don't take the lid off!
  • Leave the lid on for another 10 minutes.
Usually pork tonkatsu is served with shredded cabbage, but instead I serve it with spinach fried in lots of sesame seed oil, with garlic and a little bit of soy sauce.
  • Mince a few garlic cloves, and clean, dry, and cut the spinach if it needs it. I usually buy a 1lb bin of baby spinch that has already been washed, and that's about the right amount for four people.
  • Heat a generous amount (2 tablespoons or more) of sesame seed oil in a pan over medium-low heat and fry the garlic for about a minute. Garlic can burn and turn brown very quickly, especially if the heat is too high. I have a hard time not burning garlic, so I've started stirring it in the pan constantly, which helps.
  • Add the spinach and a little bit of soy sauce (about a packet or two) to the pan. Keep moving the spinach around so it all gets evenly wilted and then remove from heat.
My stomach turns into a vaccuum whenever I eat this - I hope you like it, too!
