Mineral Physics

Neighborite is the friendliest-sounding mineral that almost no one has heard of. However, it is a cousin/sister of the most abundant mineral in the Earth: bridgmanite. While deformation of bridgmanite (and therefore a large volume of the Earth) is very difficult to study experimentally, neighborite is much easier to work with. I sythesized neighborite, deformed it in controlled experiments while precisely measuring stress and strain, and analyzed and fit that data using Python to create a model to predict how neighborite deforms, and extrapolate to slow (about the same speed your fingernails grow) deformation constantly happening in Earth's lower mantle. To see which mechanism it deforms by, I collected electron diffraction images and analyzed the images in Matlab.
